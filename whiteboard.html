ChatÔÉÅ

Chat with collaborators. Open multiple chat boxes within the whiteboard as well as the usual CoCalc Side Chat for the entire whiteboard file.

Collaborative EditingÔÉÅ

Many users can edit at the same time, see what the others are doing in real time, and center their view at the location of another‚Äôs cursor.

Countdown TimersÔÉÅ

Add countdown timers to keep meetings and discussions on schedule.

Cut or Copy and PasteÔÉÅ

Select objects within a rectangular area and cut or copy them, then paste to the same whiteboard or a different whiteboard you already have open.

EdgesÔÉÅ

Create edges (arrows) between all objects.

FramesÔÉÅ

Use frames to group objects and organize the whiteboard into sections.

HashtagsÔÉÅ

You can put hashtags on notes, e.g. #foo and search for those as well, or put - (minus sign) in front to exclude matching items from search, e.g. -#foo. You can search for regular expressions by enclosing them in slashes, for example /^# /.

IconsÔÉÅ

Choose from a wide selection of Icons to add to your whiteboard.

Jupyter CellsÔÉÅ

A CoCalc whiteboard can include Jupyter code cells. Whiteboard code cells allow:

  • over a dozen supported kernels

  • CoCalc‚Äôs massive library of pre-installed software

  • interactive widgets

  • execution order determined by a directed graph

LaTeX ExpressionsÔÉÅ

Text inside whiteboard items supports LaTeX mathematical typesetting.

If you click the ‚ÄúPages‚Äù icon: , the Pages panel appears.

There‚Äôs a button ‚Äú+ New‚Äù that creates a new page. You can see previews of all your pages in the pages panel, and click a preview to jump to any page.

The Search panel is ordered by page number. Putting things in different pages imposes an ordering in the search.

Overview MapÔÉÅ

Easily navigate with an overview map with two preview modes.

PensÔÉÅ

Choose one of the different pen shapes to draw freehand on your whiteboard. Supported pointing devices include most forms of mouse and trackpad as well as many Wacom tablets and iPencil and Apple Pencil.

PublishÔÉÅ

You can publish your whiteboards to the CoCalc share server.

Search ViewÔÉÅ

By default, the search panel is open to the right for new whiteboards. You can also use the vertical toolbar at left and select the icon for the search panel: .

search icon in toolbar opens Search viewÔÉÅ

Search View of a whiteboard gives you a panel that lists all text, sticky notes and code in order, with a search box at the top. You can search for text and click on any matching item to center the whiteboard on it. The ordering of items in Search View is lexicographic by the (y,x) coordinates: items closer to the top are listed first; items at the same height are listed left to right.

Search view can make your whiteboards easier to manage as they get larger.

Whiteboard, showing search view in right panelÔÉÅ

Search view works well with to-do lists. To find all not done items, put "[ ]" (note the quotes) in the search box. To find all completed items, search for "[x]".

If you don‚Äôt see a menu choice for Search View in a current whiteboard, then refresh your browser, click the ‚Äúsplit vertically‚Äù button (upper right), then click the dropdown that says ‚ÄúWhiteboard‚Äù and change it to ‚ÄúSearch‚Äù.

Split Whiteboard WindowsÔÉÅ

Infinitely split whiteboard windows horizontally and vertically to view multiple parts of the whiteboard simultaneously.

Sticky NotesÔÉÅ

A sticky note is a rectangle for enclosing text. When creating a sticky note, it‚Äôs easy to select from a variety of eye-catching background colors and text fonts and sizes.

StopwatchesÔÉÅ

Add stopwatches to the whiteboard to time activities and track work.

Table of ContentsÔÉÅ

You can view contents as an outline in a panel on the left and click to view any item instantly.

TimeTravelÔÉÅ

As with other native CoCalc applications, every change is recorded via browsable Time Travel You can see what changed, and who changed it, and copy/paste from any point in the history.

To-do ListsÔÉÅ

In a sticky note, text, etc., you can mark action items by placing a pair of brackets in the item, preceded and followed by a space and with a space between the brackets. The item will be displayed with an empty checkbox. Click the checkbox when the item is done, or place an ‚Äúx‚Äù between the brackets, and it will show as a checked item.
